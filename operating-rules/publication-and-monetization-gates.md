# Publication and Monetization Gates

## Purpose

This document defines the formal gates that must be passed before a sovereign-grade digital asset may be:

- published publicly
- expanded materially
- or activated for recurring income

Its purpose is to prevent premature exposure, conceptual drift, structural weakness, and monetization-led degradation.

---

## Core Principle

Publication and monetization are not automatic rewards for activity.

They are controlled transitions.

A sovereign-grade asset should become stronger when exposed, not weaker.

---

## Gate Structure

This system uses five mandatory gates:

1. Structural Stability
2. Conceptual Integrity
3. Authority Readiness
4. Revenue Compatibility
5. Anti-Mobility Discipline

Each gate must produce one of the following outcomes:

- Pass
- Revise before proceeding
- Delay until stronger maturity
- Reject this path

---

## Gate 1: Structural Stability

### Questions

- Is the asset structurally coherent?
- Is the architecture stable and intentional?
- Can it be understood without confusion?

### Rule

If structural stability is weak, do not publish.

---

## Gate 2: Conceptual Integrity

### Questions

- Does the asset preserve its strongest strategic interpretation?
- Has the narrative become clearer or diluted?

### Rule

If conceptual integrity is weak, revise before publication.

---

## Gate 3: Authority Readiness

### Questions

- Does the asset look governed?
- Are claims precise and defensible?
- Would a serious observer take it seriously?

### Rule

If authority is weak, strengthen before exposure.

---

## Gate 4: Revenue Compatibility

### Questions

- Does monetization strengthen or weaken the asset?
- Will it damage long-term value?

### Rule

If monetization weakens authority, do not activate it.

---

## Gate 5: Anti-Mobility Discipline

### Questions

- Is this move strategically necessary?
- Or driven by impatience?

### Rule

If not necessary, delay.

---

## Output Format

- Structural Stability: <Pass / Revise / Delay / Reject>
- Conceptual Integrity: <Pass / Revise / Delay / Reject>
- Authority Readiness: <Pass / Revise / Delay / Reject>
- Revenue Compatibility: <Pass / Revise / Delay / Reject>
- Anti-Mobility Discipline: <Pass / Revise / Delay / Reject>

- Gate Summary: <short judgment>
- Recommended Next Move: <one action>

---

## Final Rule

No action should weaken:

- strategic clarity
- authority
- dignity
- long-term value

If exposure weakens the asset, it was not ready.
